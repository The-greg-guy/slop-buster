# Phrases to Remove

## Throat-Clearing Openers

Remove these announcement phrases. State the content.

- "Here's the thing:"
- "Here's what [X]"
- "Here's this [X]"
- "Here's that [X]"
- "Here's why [X]"
- "The uncomfortable truth is"
- "It turns out"
- "The real [X] is"
- "Let me be clear"
- "The truth is,"
- "I'll say it again:"
- "I'm going to be honest"
- "Can we talk about"
- "Here's what I find interesting"
- "Here's the problem though"
- "Imagine a world where"
- "Picture this:"
- "Close your eyes and"

Any "here's what/this/that" construction is throat-clearing before the point. Cut it and state the point.

## Emphasis Crutches

These add no meaning. Delete them.

- "Full stop." / "Period."
- "Let that sink in."
- "This matters because"
- "Make no mistake"
- "Here's why that matters"
- "And that's okay."
- "Think about it:"
- "Let's be real."

## Business Jargon

Replace with plain language.

| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|-------------|
| Navigate (challenges) | Handle, address |
| Unpack (analysis) | Explain, examine |
| Lean into | Accept, embrace |
| Landscape (context) | Situation, field |
| Game-changer | Significant, important |
| Double down | Commit, increase |
| Deep dive | Analysis, examination |
| Take a step back | Reconsider |
| Moving forward | Next, from now |
| Circle back | Return to, revisit |
| On the same page | Aligned, agreed |
| Synergy | Cooperation, fit |
| Paradigm shift | Change |
| Leverage | Use |
| Robust (process) | Reliable, solid |
| Scalable | Grows |
| Holistic | Complete, full |
| Iterate | Repeat, refine |
| Align (verb) | Coordinate, agree |

## AI Marketing Clichés

These phrases appear in nearly every AI-generated marketing page. Cut them.

- "Harness the power of"
- "Unlock the potential"
- "Pave the way for"
- "Setting the stage for"
- "Charting a course"
- "Navigating the complexities"
- "The dawn of a new era"
- "At the intersection of"
- "In the world of"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "The reality is"
- "In today's fast-paced world"

## Adverbs

Kill all. No -ly words. No softeners, no intensifiers, no hedges.

- "really"
- "just"
- "literally"
- "genuinely"
- "honestly"
- "simply"
- "actually"
- "deeply"
- "truly"
- "fundamentally"
- "inherently"
- "inevitably"
- "interestingly"
- "importantly"
- "crucially"
- "notably"
- "remarkably"
- "stunningly"
- "seamlessly"
- "effortlessly"
- "powerfully"
- "beautifully"
- "elegantly"

Cut these filler phrases:

- "At its core"
- "In today's [X]"
- "It's worth noting"
- "At the end of the day"
- "When it comes to"
- "In a world where"
- "The reality is"
- "In the grand scheme of things"
- "By and large"
- "For all intents and purposes"
- "Needless to say"
- "As a matter of fact"

## Meta-Commentary

Remove self-referential asides. The essay should move, not announce its own structure.

- "Hint:"
- "Plot twist:" / "Spoiler:"
- "You already know this, but"
- "But that's another post"
- "X is a feature, not a bug"
- "Dressed up as"
- "The rest of this essay explains..."
- "Let me walk you through..."
- "In this section, we'll..."
- "As we'll see..."
- "I want to explore..."
- "Before we dive in..."
- "Let's start with..."
- "First, let's consider..."
- "Now that we've covered X..."

## Performative Emphasis

False intimacy or manufactured sincerity.

- "creeps in"
- "I promise"
- "They exist, I promise"
- "Believe me"
- "Trust me on this"
- "I'll let you in on a secret"

## Telling Instead of Showing

Announcing difficulty or significance rather than demonstrating it.

- "This is genuinely hard"
- "This is what leadership actually looks like"
- "This is what X actually looks like"
- "actually matters"
- "is more than just"
- "represents a fundamental shift"

## Vague Declaratives

Sentences that announce importance without naming the specific thing. Cut these.

- "The reasons are structural"
- "The implications are significant"
- "This is the deepest problem"
- "The stakes are high"
- "The consequences are real"
- "This changes everything"
- "This is a game-changer"
- "The impact is profound"
- "This represents a paradigm shift"

If a sentence says something is important or deep or structural without showing the specific thing, cut it or replace it with the specific thing.
